SUMMARY

Concept Sketch Development is designed to allow students to solve creative problems each week, but not necessarily take every idea to its final product. This course teaches quick and agile thinking.

DESCRIPTION

The objective of this course is to focus on concept development and creative problem solving, presenting ideas in sketch form rather than fully realized pieces/products. Each week new ideas will be presented to the group as if a client were looking for a designer to take on a particular project.

COURSE METHODS

Our work in this course will be accomplished through: demonstrations, in-class studio work, lectures, image and video presentations, one-on-one instruction, group discussions/critiques, online tutorials, research and out-of-class homework. Participation is crucial to achieve the learning objectives.

EXTRA STUFF

Attendance and punctuality are mandatory. This is more true in this class than possibly any of my other graphics classes. This is because the dialog that happens in class is one of the most important things we will be doing. At times we will address assignments as a group. At other times you will be working on your own. However, I expect students to be bouncing ideas off each other, as well as me, every class. Designers often work in an isolated world, but this is not always true. Designers are just as often dealing with clients and feedback from both clients and colleagues. We teach plenty of classes where students are working alone. This will be one of your chances to work creatively in a group and I will want you to do it as often as possible.

Additionally, as with all our other classes, I expect you to behave as professionals over the course of the semester, and that includes showing up to class on time. Excused absences include athletic participation for varsity athletes, some emergencies, and documented illness. Regardless of wheth- er an absence is excused, I will not rehash lectures for students who miss class. You are responsible for gathering notes on missed classes.
